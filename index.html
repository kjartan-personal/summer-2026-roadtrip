Day 1 — Rostock

Wednesday, July 16 · Drive + ferry
ScanHotels City, Rostock
Jul 16–17 · DKK 2,063 · Confirmed
Copenhagen→Gedser→Rostock
🚗 ~170 km⚙ 2 h ferry⏱ ~4.5 h total

Route

Drive south across Sjælland to Gedser (~170 km, ~2 h). Scandlines ferry Gedser–Rostock (~2 hours). Hotel is in Rostock city centre.

Ferry booked! Gedser → Rostock, departs 13:30, arrives 15:30. The ferry has a restaurant, play area, and deck.

Charging

  • 170 km is easy on a full charge. Top up at Tesla Supercharger Rostock on arrival.
⭐Activities▼
  • Warnemünde Beach — 15 min from Rostock. Wide sandy beach, Strandkorb chairs, shallow water for kids. A lovely arrival afternoon.
  • Warnemünde Lighthouse — climb for panoramic views.
  • Alter Strom harbour — old fishing harbour with Fischbrötchen stands.
🍴Meals▼
  • Lunch: On the Scandlines ferry (buffet and café).
  • Dinner: Fischbrötchen at Warnemünde harbour, or Fischerklause on the promenade for sit-down seafood. Try Backfisch (battered fried fish).

Day 2 — Berlin (Arrive)

Thursday, July 17 · Short drive
InterContinental Berlin by IHG
Jul 17–19 · DKK 3,582 · 2 nights · Confirmed
Rostock→Berlin
🚗 ~230 km⏱ ~2.5 h⚡ Likely no stop

Route

A19 south, then A24/A10 into Berlin. Straightforward autobahn.

Charging

  • 230 km from a full charge — drive straight through. Charge at hotel or nearby Supercharger.
⭐Activities (Afternoon)▼
  • East Side Gallery — longest remaining Berlin Wall stretch (1.3 km), covered in murals. Free, stroller-friendly. Your Berlin Wall visit!
  • Gleisdreieckpark — fantastic playground on old railway tracks.
  • Brandenburg Gate & Tiergarten — iconic landmarks, big park for the kids to run.
🍴Meals▼
  • Lunch: Curry 36 (Mehringdamm) — Berlin's famous Currywurst. Or any Döner Kebab spot.
  • Dinner: Prater Garten (Prenzlauer Berg) — Berlin's oldest beer garden. Schnitzel, bratwurst, big outdoor space.

Day 3 — Berlin

Friday, July 18 · No driving
⭐Activities▼

Full day in Berlin

  • Natural History Museum — world's tallest mounted dinosaur skeleton. The 9 & 10 year olds will love it.
  • Legoland Discovery Centre (Potsdamer Platz) — indoor Lego attraction, rides, 4D cinema. ~2–3 hours.
  • Reichstag dome — free visit with audio guide. Book online in advance (free but slots fill).
  • DDR Museum — interactive museum about East Germany. Sit in a Trabant!
  • Tempelhofer Feld — former airport runway, now massive park. Rent bikes, fly kites.
🍴Meals▼
  • Lunch: Markthalle Neun (Kreuzberg) indoor food market, or Burgermeister under a U-Bahn bridge.
  • Dinner: BRLO Brwhouse (craft beer, burgers, terrace in Gleisdreieck) or Hofbräu Berlin (beer hall, schnitzel).

Day 4 — Tropical Islands

Saturday, July 19 · Short drive
Tropical Islands Resort, Krausnick
Jul 19–20 · DKK 1,885 · Confirmed

🌊 Tropical Islands — All Day + Overnight!

A massive tropical water park inside a former airship hangar — the largest free-standing building in the world. Water slides, wave pool, tropical lagoon, sandy beach, baby pool. You're staying overnight so you can enjoy it at a relaxed pace, including evening swimming!

Berlin→Tropical Islands, Krausnick
🚗 ~60 km⏱ ~45 min⚡ No charge needed

Route

A13 southeast from Berlin. Quick drive. Tesla Supercharger at the resort.

⭐Activities▼
  • Water slides — multiple slides from family-friendly to thrilling. Turbo slide, crazy river, outdoor splash area.
  • Wave pool & tropical lagoon — warm water, sandy beach, palm trees indoors.
  • Baby pool area — shallow pools and gentle features for the 1-year-old.
  • Tropical rainforest walk — real tropical plants and birds inside the dome.
  • Evening swimming — since you're staying overnight, you can swim in the evening when it's quieter. The indoor lagoon is magical with evening lighting.
  • Entry: ~€45–50/adult, ~€35–40/child. Under 3 free. Included with overnight stay? Check when booking.
🍴Meals▼
  • Multiple food courts and restaurants inside the resort. Tropical-themed dining, pizza, Asian food, burgers.
  • Breakfast included with overnight stay (check booking).

Day 5 — Dresden

Sunday, July 20 · Short drive
Boutique Hotel Rothenburger Hof, Dresden
Jul 20–21 · DKK 1,688 · Confirmed
Tropical Islands→Dresden
🚗 ~170 km⏱ ~2 h⚡ 1 charge stop possible

Route

A13 south from Krausnick, continue on A13/A4 to Dresden. Easy drive through Brandenburg and Saxony.

Charging

  • Charge at Tropical Islands Supercharger before departure, or at Tesla Supercharger Dresden on arrival.
⭐Activities (Afternoon)▼
  • Zwinger Palace — stunning baroque palace. Walk the courtyard (free), see the fountains. Old Masters Gallery inside if time permits.
  • Frauenkirche — beautifully reconstructed church. Free entry. Climb the dome for city views.
  • Brühl's Terrace — "The Balcony of Europe" — scenic walkway above the Elbe.
  • Dresden Transport Museum — trains, cars, ships, planes. The 9 & 10 year olds will love it.
  • Neustadt — cross the river to the bohemian quarter for a different vibe, street art, cafés.
🍴Meals▼
  • Lunch: Breakfast at Tropical Islands, then grab lunch at Augustiner an der Frauenkirche — Bavarian beer hall, schnitzel, pretzels.
  • Dinner: Sophienkeller — medieval-themed cellar restaurant, staff in costumes, meat on spits. Kids love it. Or explore Dresden Neustadt for international cuisine.

Route

A17/D8 south through the Ore Mountains into Czech Republic. Scenic drive through Czech-Saxon Switzerland. Short and easy.

Day 7 — Prague

Tuesday, July 22 · No driving
⭐Activities▼

Morning

  • Prague Zoo — one of the best in Europe. Huge, spread across a hillside. Gorillas, elephants, Indonesian jungle pavilion. Chair lift across the zoo. Baby-friendly. Allow 4–5 hours.

Afternoon / Evening

  • Náplavka riverbank — walk along the Vltava, paddle boats, waterfront food stalls.
  • Kampa Island — park with playgrounds, David Černý baby sculptures.
  • Walk along the Vltava at sunset. Street performers on Charles Bridge.
🍴Meals▼
  • Dinner: U Fleků — Prague's oldest brewery (est. 1499). Or Naše maso for incredible burgers.

Day 8 — Prague

Wednesday, July 23 · No driving
⭐Activities▼

Morning

  • Prague Castle — largest ancient castle complex. St. Vitus Cathedral, Golden Lane, toy museum. Arrive early (9:00) to beat crowds.

Afternoon

  • Letná Park — great playground, beer garden with panoramic river view. Walk downhill from the castle.
  • National Technical Museum — vintage cars, trains, planes. Right next to Letná Park.

Alternative / if time

  • Pedal boats on the Vltava — rent near Slav Island. Fun for the whole family.
  • Křižík Fountain — musical water & light show at the Exhibition Grounds (evening, seasonal).
🍴Meals▼
  • Lunch: Strahov Monastery Brewery — views over Prague.
  • Dinner: Eska (modern Czech) or Pivovarský dům (microbrewery, kids menu). Farewell Prague!

Day 10 — Vienna

Friday, July 25 · No driving

🎭 Wiener Prater

Vienna's legendary amusement park since 1897. Giant Ferris Wheel, roller coasters (Boomerang, Black Mamba, Megablitz, Wilde Maus), bumper cars, go-karts, ghost train. Free entry — pay per ride or buy a wristband. This is your roller coaster day!

⭐Activities▼

Morning/afternoon: Prater

  • Giant Ferris Wheel (Riesenrad) — 65m high. ~€14/adult, ~€6/child.
  • Roller coasters: Boomerang, Black Mamba, Megablitz, Wilde Maus. Mix of family and thrill.
  • For the 1-year-old: carousels, mini-trains, Lilliputbahn miniature railway.
  • Prater grounds are free. Pay per ride (€3–6) or wristband.

Alternative / afternoon

  • Haus des Meeres — aquarium in a WWII flak tower. Sharks, monkeys on rooftop.
  • Danube Island (Donauinsel) — swim in the Alte Donau. Free sandy beaches.
🍴Meals▼
  • Lunch: Prater food stands — Langos, Würstel, Leberkäse.
  • Afternoon: Café Central or Café Sacher for Sachertorte.
  • Dinner: Plachutta for Tafelspitz, or Bitzinger Würstelstand near the Albertina.

Day 13 — Leipzig

Monday, July 28 · Longest drive
NH Leipzig Zentrum
Jul 28–29 · DKK 1,371 · Confirmed
Brno→Leipzig
🚗 ~450 km⏱ ~4.5 h⚡ 1–2 charge stops

Route

D1 northwest via Prague ring road (bypass the city on the highway), then D8/A17 toward Dresden, then A14 to Leipzig. Longest drive of the trip — depart early.

Strategy: Leave Brno by 8–9 am. Charge near Prague or Dresden (~200–250 km). Arrive Leipzig by early afternoon.

Charging

  • Tesla Supercharger Prague ring or Dresden — midpoint.
  • Charge at Leipzig on arrival for tomorrow's drive.
⭐Activities (Afternoon)▼
  • Leipzig Zoo & Gondwanaland — one of Europe's best zoos. Gondwanaland is a massive indoor tropical hall with a boat ride through a rainforest. Incredible for kids. ~€22/adult, ~€14/child.
  • Augustusplatz — grand city square, walk past the opera house and university.
  • Mädler-Passage — beautiful shopping arcade with Auerbach's Keller (Faust's cellar).
🍴Meals▼
  • Dinner: Auerbachs Keller — historic cellar restaurant from Goethe's Faust. Saxon cuisine. Or Bayerischer Bahnhof — brewery in the world's oldest terminus station, famous for Gose beer and schnitzel.

Day 14 — Lübeck (Arrive)

Tuesday, July 29 · Driving day
Holiday Inn Lübeck by IHG
Jul 29–31 · DKK 3,185 · 2 nights · Confirmed
Leipzig→Lübeck
🚗 ~380 km⏱ ~3.5 h⚡ 1 charge stop

Route

A9 north toward Berlin, then A24/A1 northwest to Lübeck. Bypasses Berlin on the ring motorway.

Charging

  • Tesla Supercharger Berlin area — midpoint, ~190 km from Leipzig.
  • Charge fully in Lübeck — you need charge for Hansa Park tomorrow and the drive home.
⭐Activities (Afternoon)▼
  • Holstentor — Lübeck's iconic medieval gate. One of Germany's most recognizable landmarks.
  • Lübeck Old Town (UNESCO) — walk the charming streets, see the Marienkirche, merchant houses.
  • Niederegger Marzipan — visit the famous marzipan shop and café. Free museum upstairs. Buy marzipan to take home!
  • Trave riverside — walk along the river, harbour views.
🍴Meals▼
  • Lunch: Grab food near a charging stop, or eat on arrival.
  • Dinner: Schiffergesellschaft — historic maritime restaurant in a 16th-century guildhall. Model ships, long wooden tables. Very atmospheric. Or Fischbrötchen at the harbour.

Day 15 — Hansa Park!

Wednesday, July 30 · Short drive to park

🎭 Hansa Park — Roller Coaster Day!

Germany's only theme park by the sea, in Sierksdorf (~30 min from Lübeck). Major roller coasters, water rides, family rides, and a Baltic Sea beach. One of northern Germany's best amusement parks.

Lübeck→Hansa Park, Sierksdorf
🚗 ~30 km⏱ ~30 min
⭐Activities▼
  • Drive ~30 min north to Hansa Park, Sierksdorf.
  • Kärnan — 127m tall coaster with an indoor free-fall drop. The flagship ride. Height min 130 cm.
  • Nessie — classic looping coaster. A family favourite.
  • Fluch von Novgorod — launched dark ride coaster. Intense and amazing.
  • Der kleine Zar, Barentsee, Schlange von Midgard — family rides the 9 & 10 year olds can enjoy.
  • Water rides: Wildwasserbahn (log flume), Sturmfahrt (rapids).
  • For the 1-year-old: Kinderland area with gentle rides, carousels, play areas.
  • Entry: ~€45–50/person. Under 3 free. Book online at hansapark.de.
  • Beach: Hansa Park is right on the Baltic Sea — there's a beach area in the park!
Full day: Arrive at opening (~9:00), do the big coasters first before queues build. Eat lunch in the park. Stay until closing.
🍴Meals▼
  • Lunch: In the park — various themed restaurants and food stands.
  • Dinner: Back in Lübeck. Try Kartoffelkeller (potato-themed restaurant, kids love it) or Niederegger Café for marzipan dessert.

Tesla Standard Range RWD
